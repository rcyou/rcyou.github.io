Bliss Events & Staffing

Web app design for an event staffing company.
  • Brief: Create an app to help manage and fill event staffing needs.
  • Role: Designer
  • Agency: Visionary Services

In short, Bliss is like Uber for event staffing in Iowa. Primarily focusing on weddings and private events, Bliss manages a few dozen subcontractors around the state who sign up to work as kitchen help, bartenders, wait staff, or other various positions as assigned. At Visionary Services, I am working on a new mobile web application for Bliss to manage staff and staffing requests, designing the UX process flows and creating the UI.

UX Challenges

Staffing is run through one person at Bliss, generally initiated via text message or email and then managed in a series of Google docs. Since the subcontractors are used to direct communication, there needed to be a way to continue that direct contact but also automate the process. In turn, the experience of using a new tool needs to feel natural for workers being asked to change their processes.

Process

With limited visible screen real estate on mobile, prioritization of content was my first step in designing the app interface. Low-fidelity, grayscale wireframes were my tool of choice since it allowed me to focus on visual hierarchy and affordance right away. I also wanted to be able to print out the screens to test font size and readability since certain interactions and prompts would have limited but important content.

After establishing a baseline with just a few screens, I went back to hashing out the process flow and understanding what the key user actions were: accepting a job, declining a job, and viewing a job but no accepting or declining. To me, it was important to let users decide their schedule because they are subcontractors. Otherwise, they should just be assigned a job.

I also looked at where a user could potentially veer off, listed possible reasons or scenarios, and presented the options for my team to determine if some routes could be closed.Meanwhile, I created more of the key wireframes and did a combination of paper prototyping and user testing to check my design assumptions with others in the office.

To keep our client looped into the process, I started creating high-fidelity mockups to provide a sense of visual direction for the app, which will coincide with the redesign for the Bliss marketing website where staffing requests will start.
